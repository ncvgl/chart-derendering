================================================================================
INSIGHTS — ChartMuseum Visual QA Experiments (April 9-10, 2026)
================================================================================

FINAL RESULTS
  Sonnet + Read,Bash:       60/83 (72.3%)  — best overall
  Sonnet + Devils Advocate: 45/83 (54.2%)  — best reliable (Read-only)
  Sonnet + Read only:       44/83 (53.0%)  — baseline
  Sonnet + Decompose:       40/83 (48.2%)  — hurt easy questions

KEY FINDINGS

1. Tools > prompt engineering. Read+Bash adds +19% over Read-only (72% vs 53%).
   All prompt-only techniques (decompose, devil's advocate) cluster at 40-54%.
   The crop tool lets the model zoom into chart regions at native resolution,
   which is the single biggest accuracy driver.

2. ~20% of answers are stochastic. On identical reruns, ~2/10 questions flip
   between correct/incorrect. Overall score stays stable but individual answers
   vary. (Observed: sonnet_read v1 and v2 both scored 7/10 but on different questions.)

3. Legend misidentification is the #1 failure mode. Models assume colors
   (e.g. pink=spirits) without checking the legend. When they verify via
   cropping the legend, accuracy jumps. q13 (Costa Rica): all models say Cuba
   until they crop and read the legend — then they self-correct.

4. Claude downsamples images >1568px on the long edge. Charts wider than
   1568px lose detail. The crop tool bypasses this by extracting sub-regions
   at native resolution — "zoom with intent."

5. Sonnet > Opus on this benchmark. Sonnet+Read (70% on 10-subset) beats
   Opus+Read (60%). Opus effort levels don't help: low=40%, default=60%, max=50%.

6. Haiku cannot self-correct. With Bash tools, Sonnet verifies legends and
   fixes color misidentifications (2/3 on q13). Haiku never checks the legend
   and gets q13 wrong 3/3 times despite having the same tools.

APPROACH-SPECIFIC FINDINGS

7. Devil's advocate (two-pass challenge): Flips ~2/10 hard failures by forcing
   the model to consider alternatives. Simple, Read-only, near-zero timeouts.
   But also flips ~11/83 correct answers to wrong (net +6 over baseline).

8. Structured decomposition ("describe then answer"): Best on 10 hardest
   failures (4/10) but hurts easy questions at scale (+1 net over baseline).
   The description step sometimes introduces errors.

9. Opus critic pattern (Sonnet answers → Opus critiques → Sonnet revises):
   Works when Opus has image access and can point to specific visual elements.
   Text-only critic catches reasoning errors but Sonnet can't act on vague
   advice. Tested on q28 (range vs average) and q6 (NBA playoffs) — both
   corrected.

INFRASTRUCTURE FINDINGS

10. subprocess.communicate(timeout) doesn't kill child process trees on macOS.
    Claude CLI spawns MCP servers/node processes that survive the timeout.
    Fix: Popen(start_new_session=True) + os.killpg() + drain pipes after kill.

11. 300s timeout is too short for Read+Bash. Complex charts take 20-40 turns
    of crop/read (up to 500s). 600s timeout with 2 retries and 10s/20s backoff
    eliminates most timeouts.

12. Some large images (>2MB, >2500px) consistently stall with Bash tool
    initialization. Read-only works fine on the same images. Use Read-only
    fallback for persistent Bash stallers.

13. Rate limiting at high parallelism causes stall timeouts (0 events, CLI
    never initializes). Parallelism 2-3 is the sweet spot. Parallelism 5
    causes 22/83 stalls vs 9/83 with Read-only.

PER-QUESTION CASE STUDIES

q13 (countryDrink.png — Costa Rica): Legend verification is critical. Without
    it, all models say Cuba (wrong color mapping). With Bash + 24 turns,
    Sonnet crops legend, corrects colors, and finds Costa Rica. Haiku can't
    do this — wrong color mapping in all 3 attempts.

q28 (VOTD.png — Friday): Models confuse "range" (spread) with "average" and
    read Tuesday=508 from the radar chart. Opus critic with image access
    points to the trajectory arcs — Sonnet then analyzes individual throws
    and finds Friday has the widest spread. 31 turns, 234s.

q6  (NBAmissPlayOffs.png — 4 teams): Consistently stalls with Bash but
    Opus critic with image access got it correct on retry.
